Microbiology at a Glance

Microbiology which is the study of microorganism is defined not only by the size of its subjects but the techniques it uses to study them. Microbiology has both basic and applied aspects. The basic aspect are concerned with the biology of Microorganisms themselves and include such fields as bacteriology, virology, mycology, phycology, microbial cytology and physiology, microbial genetics and molecular biology, microbial ecology and microbial taxonomy. The applied aspect are concerned with practical problems such as disease, water and waste water treatment, food spoilage and food production and industrial uses of microbes.

Aspect of microbiology are however intertwined. It differs from other aspect aspect of biology because it deals with structure, function and application of microorganism to our world. Basic research is often conducted in applied fields and applications often arise out of the basic research.

Some of the most existing aspect of modern microbiology research include;

  • Medical Microbiology
  • Public Health Microbiology
  • Immunology
  • Microbial Physiology
  • Microbial Genetics
  • Molecular biology and Genetic engineering
The discovery of new and unusual microorganisms and creation of genetically modified organism (GMO) will also lead to further advances in the development of new antimicrobial angent, industrial process and bioremediation.
